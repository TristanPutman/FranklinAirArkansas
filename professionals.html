Understanding HVAC Design

Custom AC Sizing & Duct Design

These industry-standard calculations are the foundation of a properly designed HVAC system. Most states require them for building permits.

Precision Load Calculation

Custom AC Sizing

Determines exactly how much heating and cooling your home needs based on its size, insulation, windows, orientation, climate zone, and more. This is the starting point for proper HVAC sizing — no more guessing.

  • Room-by-room BTU calculations
  • Accounts for insulation, windows, and climate
  • Prevents oversized or undersized equipment
  • Required for building permits in most states

Custom Duct Design

Airflow Engineering

A custom ductwork design that ensures the right amount of airflow reaches every room. Includes trunk duct sizing, branch duct sizing, register locations, and a scaled drawing of the complete system.

  • Custom scaled duct layout drawings
  • Trunk and branch duct sizing
  • Register locations and sizes
  • Ensures balanced airflow throughout

Equipment Verification

Right-Sized Selection

Certifies that your selected AC and heating equipment properly matches the calculated load for your home. Expressed in sensible and latent BTU, this ensures your system is the right fit — not too big, not too small.

  • Equipment-to-load verification
  • Sensible and latent capacity analysis
  • Ensures proper equipment sizing
  • Required for permit compliance

FAQ

Common Questions

Do I really need a professional load calculation?

Yes — in most states, a certified load calculation is required to obtain a building permit for new construction or major HVAC replacements. Even where not required, it ensures your system is properly sized for maximum comfort and efficiency. An oversized system wastes energy and creates humidity problems, while an undersized system can’t keep up on extreme days.

What information do you need from me?

We typically need your floor plans (PDF or images), the home’s square footage, location/address, insulation details, window types, and any specific equipment preferences. Don’t worry if you don’t have everything — we’ll walk you through what’s needed during your free consultation.

How long does it take to get my report?

Standard turnaround is 5–7 business days after we receive all required information. Rush service is available for an additional fee if you need it faster. We’ll give you a clear timeline when you place your order.

Are your reports accepted for building permits?

Absolutely. Our reports follow ACCA-compliant methodology and are accepted in all 50 states. We provide code-ready documentation that building inspectors expect to see.

What’s included in a complete HVAC design?

A Load Calculation determines how much heating/cooling your home needs. A Duct Design engineers the ductwork to deliver that air properly. Equipment Verification confirms that your specific system matches those needs. For new construction, we recommend all three for a complete, permit-ready design.

Do you offer commercial HVAC design?

Yes. We handle commercial load calculations and duct design for multi-family buildings, offices, retail spaces, and more. Commercial projects are quoted individually based on scope — call us to discuss your project.
